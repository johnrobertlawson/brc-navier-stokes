## O2607-01: exact profile quantifiers

Let

\[
I=(T^*-\epsilon,T^*),
\qquad
\omega(x,t)=|\boldsymbol\omega(x,t)|.
\]

A source-compatible exact reading of L87–89 requires a fixed centre
\(a\in\mathbb R^3\), constants \(M_\Phi,M_{\nabla\Phi}<\infty\), and a nonnegative
shape factor such that, for almost every \(x\ne a\) and every \(t\in I\),

\[
\omega(x,t)=\Phi(x,t)|x-a|^{-2},
\qquad
0\le\Phi(x,t)\le M_\Phi,
\qquad
|\nabla_x\Phi(x,t)|
\le M_{\nabla\Phi}|x-a|^{-1}.
\]

The proof never uses the gradient bound, scale invariance, or log periodicity.
Its minimal profile hypothesis is instead:

\[
A_\lambda(t)
\subset
B_{C_{\mathrm{prof}}\lambda^{-1/2}}(a)
\quad
\text{for all }t\in I,\ \lambda\ge\lambda_{\mathrm{prof}},
\]

with \(C_{\mathrm{prof}}\) and \(\lambda_{\mathrm{prof}}\) independent of time,
together with the uniform weak-Lorentz bound stated separately in L233 and L635.

### Verified implications

If \(0\le\Phi\le M_\Phi\) globally, then

\[
\omega(x,t)>\lambda
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
|x-a|<\sqrt{M_\Phi}\lambda^{-1/2}.
\]

Thus \(C_{\mathrm{prof}}=\sqrt{M_\Phi}\), and

\[
|\{\omega(\cdot,t)>\lambda\}|
\le
\frac{4\pi}{3}M_\Phi^{3/2}\lambda^{-3/2}.
\]

Under the source's rearrangement normalisation,

\[
\|\omega(\cdot,t)\|_{L^{3/2,\infty}}
\le
\left(\frac{4\pi}{3}\right)^{2/3}M_\Phi.
\]

If the factorization is intended only on \(B_{r_0}(a)\), the same conclusions require
a uniform exterior bound \(M_{\mathrm{out}}\) and only hold at levels
\(\lambda>\max(M_{\mathrm{out}},M_\Phi r_0^{-2})\).

### Adversarial tests

The exact

\]

yet any critical-radius covering constant satisfies

isotropy follows from weak-\(L^{3/2}\).

They do not satisfy the repaired bounded fixed-centre envelope and therefore do not
refute the conditional theorem.

## O2607-02: direction at zeros

L69 defines
\(\boldsymbol\xi=\boldsymbol\omega/|\boldsymbol\omega|\), which is undefined on
\[
Z_t=\{x:|\boldsymbol\omega(x,t)|=0\}.
\]

L99–104 then takes global ball averages of \(\boldsymbol\xi\), and L233 and L635 take
its global weighted-BMO norm. The minimal repair is the following explicit theorem
hypothesis:

> There exists a jointly measurable
> \(\boldsymbol\xi:\mathbb R^3\times I\to\mathbb S^2\) such that
> \(\boldsymbol\omega=|\boldsymbol\omega|\boldsymbol\xi\) almost everywhere and
> \[
> M_\xi=
> \operatorname*{ess\,sup}_{t\in I}
> \|\boldsymbol\xi(\cdot,t)\|_{\mathrm{bmo}_{1/|\log r|}}
> <\infty.
> \]

On \(\mathbb R^3\setminus Z_t\), this fixes the usual direction. On \(Z_t\), the
extension is part of the hypothesis and must be chosen before the proof. A change on
a measure-zero zero set does not change any BMO average; a change on a positive-measure
zero set can.

The source factor
\(|\boldsymbol\omega|\boldsymbol\xi=\boldsymbol\omega\) is independent of the
extension. Evaluation points in \(A_\lambda(t)\), with \(\lambda>0\), also have a
canonical direction. The extension nevertheless enters \(c_R\) and every BMO
oscillation estimate at L252–367, so its norm cannot be inferred after the
commutator is formed.

The
[zero-set direction example](../experiments/zero-set-direction.md) constructs a
smooth compactly supported divergence-free vorticity which equals
\((x_1,-x_2,0)\) near the origin. Its normalised direction has mean oscillation
exactly \(1\) on every centred small ball, and no assignment on the zero axis can put
it in \(\mathrm{bmo}_{1/|\log r|}\). Thus the repaired extension hypothesis is a
genuine restriction, not a consequence of smoothness.

## O2607-04: exact tensor calculation

The complete human-verifiable derivation is recorded in the
[strain-cancellation certificate](../experiments/strain-cancellation.md). With
\(z=x-y\) and \(\varepsilon_{123}=1\), it gives

\[
S_{i\ell}(x)
=
\frac{3}{8\pi}
\operatorname{p.v.}
\int
\frac{
  (z\times\boldsymbol\omega(y))_i z_\ell
  +z_i(z\times\boldsymbol\omega(y))_\ell
}{|z|^5}\,dy,
\]

and, for \(e=\boldsymbol\xi(x)\),

\[
\alpha(x)
=
\frac{3}{4\pi}
\operatorname{p.v.}
\int
\frac{
  |\boldsymbol\omega(y)|
  (e\cdot z)
  \bigl(z\cdot(\boldsymbol\xi(y)\times e)\bigr)
}{|z|^5}\,dy.
\]

The fixed-direction contribution vanishes because \(e\times e=0\). If
\(T_{i\ell j}\) maps a scalar source in vorticity component \(j\) to
\(S_{i\ell}\), the exact commutator identity is

\[
\alpha(x)
=
\sum_{i,\ell,j}
e_i e_\ell
\left[
  T_{i\ell j},\xi_j
\right]
|\boldsymbol\omega|(x).
\]
