# Bridge note: certified Leray–Hopf nonuniqueness

**Paper:** Thomas Hou, Yixuan Wang, and Changhe Yang, *Nonuniqueness of
Leray–Hopf solutions to the unforced incompressible 3D Navier–Stokes Equation*
**Source:** [arXiv:2509.25116v2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.25116)
**Version date:** 2026-03-19
**Status:** preprint claim; independent reproduction not yet completed

## What the paper reports

The authors report a rigorous computer-assisted proof of an exact forward self-similar
profile for unforced 3D Navier–Stokes, together with an unstable eigenpair for the
linearized similarity operator. After localization and a fixed-point construction, they
claim infinitely many suitable Leray–Hopf solutions sharing compactly supported critical
initial data. The solutions are smooth for positive times.

The computational architecture separates:

1. a high-accuracy numerical profile;
2. analytic residual bounds;
3. a coercive-plus-compact operator decomposition;
4. finite-rank approximation of the compact part;
5. interval/certified spectral bounds;
6. a nonlinear fixed-point argument yielding a nearby exact object.

This is exactly the separation the proof lab should reproduce.

## Why this does not settle Clay

The initial data are critical and singular at the initial time. The paper explicitly
warns readers not to confuse forward self-similarity from singular data with backward
self-similar formation of a finite-time singularity from smooth data.

Clay alternatives A–D begin with smooth data. Smoothness of these solutions for every
positive time does not retroactively make their common initial data smooth. Nor does
nonuniqueness at this rough threshold prove failure of a classical solution arising from
smooth data.

## Why it matters to the moonshot

- It supplies a serious template for computer-assisted infinite-dimensional PDE proofs.
- It identifies an exact instability mechanism rather than a merely visual numerical
  candidate.
- It creates a concrete bridge problem: can such instability be reached from an allowed
  smooth prehistory or embedded using a smooth Clay-admissible force?
- It can also support regularity: proving that smooth evolutions cannot enter the
  certified unstable manifold would eliminate a breakdown route.

## Reproduction protocol

1. Pin the paper, repository revision, toolchain, and all generated coefficient files.
2. Recompute the approximate profile independently.
3. Verify exact divergence-free structure and far-field asymptotics.
4. Recompute residual bounds using directed rounding.
5. Rebuild the finite-rank operator and spectral enclosure through an independent code
   path.
6. Check that every numerical constant used by the analytic fixed-point proof is read
   from a certificate rather than copied from prose.
7. Perturb resolution, basis, domain compactification, and quadrature order while
   retaining rigorous enclosures.

## Bridge research questions

### Smooth prehistory

Can any state on or near the unstable similarity manifold arise at a positive time from
a smooth earlier state? Backward parabolic evolution is ill-posed, so numerical backward
integration is not evidence. The target must be an exact matching or invariant-manifold
theorem.

### Smooth forcing

Can the localization error or a controlled trajectory be realized by a force satisfying
Fefferman's exact smoothness and decay/periodicity conditions, while producing failure
of global smoothness rather than only weak nonuniqueness?

### Exclusion

Does weak–strong uniqueness or another rigidity principle prevent a smooth strong branch
from reaching the nonunique rough regime? A rigorous “cannot bridge” result would close
part of the breakdown tree and sharpen the regularity target.

## Current verdict

Treat this as a high-value certified mechanism and an open bridge, not a prize solution.
The first task is faithful independent reproduction; extension comes afterward.
