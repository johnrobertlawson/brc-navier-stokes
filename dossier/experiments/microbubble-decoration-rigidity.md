## 1. Joint terminal--interior Young measure

Let \(Q\) be the fixed parabolic cylinder selected by the microbubble theorem.
Write

\[
A_j(y,s)
=
\widetilde H_j(y,0),
\qquad
B_j(y,s)
=
\widetilde H_j(y,s).
\tag{1}
\]

Both tensors take values in

\[
\mathcal P
=
\left\{
H\in\operatorname{Sym}_3:
H\ge0,\ 
\operatorname{tr}H\le1
\right\},
\tag{2}
\]

a compact convex set. Define a measure on
\(Q\times\mathcal P\times\mathcal P\) by

\[
\int\Phi\,d\Upsilon_j
=
\int_Q
\Phi(y,s,A_j(y,s),B_j(y,s))\,dy\,ds.
\tag{3}
\]

Its \(Q\)-marginal is Lebesgue measure and its total mass is fixed. Compactness
therefore gives, after a subsequence,

\[
\Upsilon_j\rightharpoonup^*\Upsilon.
\tag{4}
\]

Disintegration writes

\[
d\Upsilon
=
dy\,ds\,d\upsilon_{y,s}(A,B),
\tag{5}
\]

where \(\upsilon_{y,s}\) is a probability measure on
\(\mathcal P^2\) for almost every \((y,s)\).
