## 5. Two distinct zero-ratio boundaries

The one-radius formulation described only \(q_k\to0\). Equation (16) shows
that this conflates two different losses.

### Internal boundary: \(\lambda_k\to0\)

The carrier is a tangent below its parent detector scale. Its intrinsic
strain and squared detector have weights

\[
\lambda_k^2
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
\lambda_k^4,
\tag{34}
\]

so the order-one \(D_k\) mark is not intrinsic carrier PDE data. Weak
convergence of the parent on fixed \(R_k\)-coordinates does not determine the
carrier tangent at \(\lambda=0\).

### Inter-event boundary: \(\kappa_k\to0\)

Even after the \(k\)-th carrier is known, the next parent can lie at an
unresolved smaller subscale. Weak convergence on fixed \(r_k\)-coordinates
does not determine that next parent at \(\kappa=0\).

The derived ratios \(q_k=\lambda_k\kappa_k\) and
\(\rho_k=\kappa_k\lambda_{k+1}\) can vanish for different reasons. A
single \(q=0\) mark cannot recover which edge lost compactness or how the
external detector is transported. Any no-neck theorem must specify the
two-edge graph it closes.
