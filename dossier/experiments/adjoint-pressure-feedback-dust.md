The reviewed source-localised branch provides

\[
G_h
=
\nabla\Delta^{-1}\operatorname{div}
\bigl((r\cdot\nabla)b^{\rm in}\bigr),
\qquad
\int_0^h\|G_h(\tau)\|_1\,d\tau\ge p_{\rm in}>0,
\]

with

\[
\operatorname{supp}b^{\rm in}\subset B_{8h^{-3}},
\qquad
\|r(\tau)\|_2\lesssim\tau,
\qquad
\int_0^h\|\nabla r\|_2^2\,d\tau\lesssim h^2.
\]

Its pressure has a fixed high-pass fraction above

\[
K_h=\kappa h^{-1/2}.
\]

This note shows that the positive-clock descendant cannot be retained by
one ordinary centred compactness zoom. The local pressure payment first
forces

\[
\int_0^h\|\nabla b^{\rm in}\|_2^2\,d\tau
\asymp h^{-3}.
\]

Thus it saturates the full scale-critical local-energy allowance of the
inverse-cubic source ball.

There is then a sharper exhaustive alternative. For fixed constants
\(a_*>0\) and \(d_*>0\), independent of \(h\), either

\[
\boxed{
\int_0^h
\left\|
\nabla(I-S_{a_*K_h})b^{\rm in}
\right\|_2^2\,d\tau
\ge d_*h^{-3},
}
\]

or a fixed pressure fraction is generated by factors below fixed
multiples of \(K_h\). In the latter branch, every union of
\(K_h^{-1}\)-cubes capturing that fixed pressure fraction contains at
least

\[
\boxed{
N_{\rm pressure}(h)\gtrsim h^{-21/2}
}
\]

cubes. This is the same power as the total number of descendant cubes in
the inverse-cubic source ball:

\[
\left(h^{-3}K_h\right)^3\asymp h^{-21/2}.
\]

In the high-frequency branch, the corresponding actual coefficient
dissipation already needs at least

\[
\boxed{
N_{\rm diss}(h)\gtrsim h^{-7/2}
}
\]

descendant cubes.

On the physical trajectory, with

\[
\lambda=\sigma h^{-3},
\qquad
\mu=\frac{\sigma}{K_h}
=\kappa^{-1}\sigma\sqrt h,
\]

these counts are

\[
N_{\rm diss}\gtrsim\frac{\lambda}{\mu},
\qquad
N_{\rm pressure}\gtrsim
\left(\frac{\lambda}{\mu}\right)^3.
\]

The finite-band branch is therefore maximally delocalised in volume,
while the other branch retains a fixed fraction of the entire local
dissipation budget above the descendant frequency. Neither produces a
single nonzero pressure-marked suitable profile.

## 1. Reviewed source-localised input

Set

\[
R:=h^{-3},
\tag{1}
\]

and retain

\[
b^{\rm in}:=\chi_Rb,
\qquad
\chi_R=1\ \hbox{on }B_{4R},
\qquad
\operatorname{supp}\chi_R\subset B_{8R},
\qquad
|\nabla\chi_R|\le CR^{-1}.
\tag{2}
\]

The reviewed local-payer branch gives

\[
\boxed{
\int_0^h\|G_h(\tau)\|_1\,d\tau
\ge p_{\rm in}>0,
}
\tag{3}
\]

where

\[
G_h
:=
\mathcal T(r,b^{\rm in})
=
\nabla\Delta^{-1}\operatorname{div}
\bigl((r\cdot\nabla)b^{\rm in}\bigr).
\tag{4}
\]

The zero-data feedback bounds are

\[
\boxed{
\int_0^h\|r(\tau)\|_2^2\,d\tau
\le C_rh^3,
}
\tag{5}
\]

\[
\boxed{
\int_0^h\|\nabla r(\tau)\|_2^2\,d\tau
\le C_rh^2.
}
\tag{6}
\]

The coefficient satisfies

\[
\sup_{0\le\tau\le h}
\|b(\tau)\|_{L^{3,\infty}}\le M,
\tag{7}
\]

and the centre-uniform local-energy estimate

\[
\boxed{
\int_0^h\int_{B_s(y)}
|\nabla b|^2\,dx\,d\tau
\le C_{\rm LE}s
}
\tag{8}
\]

whenever

\[
\nu h\le\delta_{\rm LE}s^2.
\tag{9}
\]

The Hardy div--curl estimate is

\[
\boxed{
\int_0^h
\|\mathcal T(z,c)(\tau)\|_1\,d\tau
\le
C_{\rm dH}
\left(
\int_0^h\|z\|_2^2\,d\tau
\right)^{1/2}
\left(
\int_0^h\|\nabla c\|_2^2\,d\tau
\right)^{1/2}
}
\tag{10}
\]

for solenoidal \(z\). The same estimate holds with the \(L^1\) norm
replaced by the real Hardy-space norm.

## 2. The inner coefficient saturates local energy

Define

\[
D_{\rm in}(h)
:=
\int_0^h\|\nabla b^{\rm in}(\tau)\|_2^2\,d\tau.
\tag{11}
\]

The cutoff error obeys

\[
\begin{aligned}
\int_0^h
\|b\nabla\chi_R\|_2^2\,d\tau
&\le
CR^{-2}
\int_0^h
\|b\|_{L^2(B_{8R})}^2\,d\tau\\
&\le
CM^2hR^{-1}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{12}
\]

Here finite-volume weak-\(L^3\) embedding gives

\[
\|b(\tau)\|_{L^2(B_{8R})}^2
\le CM^2R.
\tag{13}
\]

Equations (8), (12), and \(R=h^{-3}\) imply

\[
\boxed{
D_{\rm in}(h)
\le C_{\rm in}\left(R+hR^{-1}\right)
\le C_{\rm in}h^{-3}.
}
\tag{14}
\]

Conversely, (3), (5), and (10) give

\[
p_{\rm in}
\le
C_{\rm dH}C_r^{1/2}
h^{3/2}D_{\rm in}(h)^{1/2}.
\tag{15}
\]

Therefore

\[
\boxed{
c_{\rm in}h^{-3}
\le
D_{\rm in}(h)
\le
C_{\rm in}h^{-3}.
}
\tag{16}
\]

This is a two-sided saturation theorem, not merely a lower dissipation
power.

The cutoff cannot pay the lower bound. Indeed, (12) is \(O(h^4)\), so
from

\[
\|\nabla(\chi_Rb)\|_2^2
\le
2\|\chi_R\nabla b\|_2^2
+2\|b\nabla\chi_R\|_2^2
\tag{17}
\]

one obtains, for all sufficiently small \(h\),

\[
\boxed{
\int_0^h\int_{B_{8R}}
|\nabla b|^2\,dx\,d\tau
\ge c_{\rm loc}h^{-3}.
}
\tag{18}
\]

## 3. The positive-clock grid

Reduce the reviewed constant \(\kappa>0\), if necessary, so that

\[
\nu\kappa^2\le\delta_{\rm LE}.
\tag{19}
\]

This only strengthens the reviewed high-pass lower bound. Put

\[
K:=K_h=\kappa h^{-1/2},
\qquad
\ell:=K^{-1}=\kappa^{-1}\sqrt h.
\tag{20}
\]

Then (9) holds for every ball of radius at least \(\ell\).

Let

\[
\mathcal Q_\ell
:=
\left\{
Q_m=\ell\bigl(m+[0,1)^3\bigr):
m\in\mathbb Z^3
\right\}.
\tag{21}
\]

Every cube \(Q_m\) lies in a ball of radius \(C\ell\), so (8) gives

\[
\boxed{
\int_0^h\int_{Q_m}|\nabla b|^2\,dx\,d\tau
\le C_{\rm cell}\ell.
}
\tag{22}
\]

Combining (18) and (22), every collection of grid cubes capturing half
of the dissipation in \(B_{8R}\) has cardinality at least

\[
\boxed{
N_{\rm diss}(h)
\ge
c\frac{h^{-3}}{\ell}
=cKh^{-3}
\asymp h^{-7/2}.
}
\tag{23}
\]

This lower bound holds throughout the source-localised branch, before
any frequency split.

## 4. Factor-frequency split

Let \(S_L\) denote a smooth radial low-pass with symbol
\(\chi(\xi/L)\), using the same cutoff as in the reviewed frequency
theorem. Fix a large constant \(A>1\), to be chosen independently of
\(h\), and write

\[
r=r^{\rm lo}+r^{\rm hi},
\qquad
r^{\rm lo}:=S_{AK}r,
\tag{24}
\]

\[
b^{\rm in}=b^{\rm lo}+b^{\rm hi},
\qquad
b^{\rm lo}:=S_Kb^{\rm in}.
\tag{25}
\]

Define the localised high-frequency coefficient energy

\[
E_{\rm hi}(h)
:=
\int_0^h
\|\nabla b^{\rm hi}(\tau)\|_2^2\,d\tau.
\tag{26}
\]

Let

\[
P_{>K}:=I-S_K.
\tag{27}
\]

Smooth Littlewood--Paley multipliers are bounded on the real Hardy
space. Using (6), (14), and the high-frequency Bernstein estimate gives

\[
\begin{aligned}
\int_0^h
\left\|
P_{>K}\mathcal T(r^{\rm hi},b^{\rm in})
\right\|_1\,d\tau
&\le
C
\left(
\int_0^h\|r^{\rm hi}\|_2^2
\right)^{1/2}
D_{\rm in}^{1/2}\\
&\le
\frac{C}{AK}
\left(
\int_0^h\|\nabla r\|_2^2
\right)^{1/2}
D_{\rm in}^{1/2}\\
&\le
\frac{C_\kappa}{A}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{28}
\]

Similarly, (5) gives

\[
\boxed{
\int_0^h
\left\|
P_{>K}\mathcal T(r^{\rm lo},b^{\rm hi})
\right\|_1\,d\tau
\le
C h^{3/2}E_{\rm hi}(h)^{1/2}.
}
\tag{29}
\]

The reviewed theorem supplies

\[
\boxed{
\int_0^h
\|P_{>K}G_h(\tau)\|_1\,d\tau
\ge p_{\rm hf}>0.
}
\tag{30}
\]

Choose \(A\) so large that the right-hand side of (28) is at most
\(p_{\rm hf}/4\), and then choose \(d_*>0\) so small that

\[
C\sqrt{d_*}\le\frac{p_{\rm hf}}4.
\tag{31}
\]

There are now two exhaustive possibilities.

### 4.1. Frequency-cascade branch

\[
\boxed{
E_{\rm hi}(h)\ge d_*h^{-3}.
}
\tag{32}
\]

This is a fixed fraction of the complete upper bound (14). It is
localised-coefficient frequency energy above the fixed multiple \(K\);
no claim about a sharp Fourier support of the uncut coefficient is made.

### 4.2. Finite-factor-frequency branch

If (32) fails, (28)--(31) and the triangle inequality give

\[
\boxed{
\int_0^h
\|H_h(\tau)\|_1\,d\tau
\ge\frac{p_{\rm hf}}2,
}
\tag{33}
\]

where

\[
H_h
:=
P_{>K}
\mathcal T(r^{\rm lo},b^{\rm lo}).
\tag{34}
\]

The factors in (34) have Fourier support below fixed multiples of
\(K\). Hence \(H_h\) is supported in one fixed annulus

\[
K\lesssim|\xi|\lesssim C_AK.
\tag{35}
\]

Equivalently, (34) is a convolution of
\((r^{\rm lo}\cdot\nabla)b^{\rm lo}\) with a kernel \(L_K\) satisfying,
for every \(N_0\),

\[
\boxed{
|L_K(x)|
\le
C_{A,N_0}K^3(1+K|x|)^{-N_0}.
}
\tag{36}
\]

## 5. Vector-valued spatial capture estimate

Let \(\mathcal F\subset\mathbb Z^3\) be finite, put

\[
U_{\mathcal F}
:=
\bigcup_{m\in\mathcal F}Q_m,
\qquad
N:=|\mathcal F|,
\tag{37}
\]

and define

\[
w_{\mathcal F}(y)
:=
\int_{U_{\mathcal F}}|L_K(x-y)|\,dx.
\tag{38}
\]

The kernel bound (36) gives

\[
\|w_{\mathcal F}\|_\infty\le C_A,
\qquad
\|w_{\mathcal F}\|_1\le C_AN\ell^3.
\tag{39}
\]

Real interpolation therefore yields

\[
\boxed{
\|w_{\mathcal F}\|_{L^{3,1}}
\le
C_A N^{1/3}\ell.
}
\tag{40}
\]

From (36)--(38),

\[
\begin{aligned}
\int_0^h\int_{U_{\mathcal F}}|H_h|
&\le
C_A
\int_0^h\int_{\mathbb R^3}
|r^{\rm lo}|
|\nabla b^{\rm lo}|
w_{\mathcal F}\,dx\,d\tau\\
&\le
C_A
\left(
\int_0^h\int|r^{\rm lo}|^2w_{\mathcal F}
\right)^{1/2}
\left(
\int_0^h\int|\nabla b^{\rm lo}|^2w_{\mathcal F}
\right)^{1/2}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{41}
\]

The first factor in (41) is bounded using (5) and (39):

\[
\int_0^h\int|r^{\rm lo}|^2w_{\mathcal F}
\le C_Ah^3.
\tag{42}
\]

Low-pass boundedness and Lorentz--Bernstein give

\[
\|\nabla b^{\rm lo}(\tau)\|_{L^{3,\infty}}
\le C_AKM.
\tag{43}
\]

Since

\[
L^{3/2,\infty}\cdot L^{3,1}\longrightarrow L^1,
\tag{44}
\]

equations (40) and (43) imply

\[
\begin{aligned}
\int_0^h\int
|\nabla b^{\rm lo}|^2w_{\mathcal F}
&\le
C_AM^2hK^2N^{1/3}\ell\\
&=
C_AM^2hK N^{1/3}\\
&\le
C_{A,\kappa,M}h^{1/2}N^{1/3}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{45}
\]

Substitution into (41) gives the vector-valued capture estimate

\[
\boxed{
\int_0^h\int_{U_{\mathcal F}}|H_h|\,dx\,d\tau
\le
C_{\rm cap}h^{7/4}N^{1/6}.
}
\tag{46}
\]

Because \(H_h\in L^1_{t,x}\), some finite collection of grid cubes
captures at least \(p_{\rm hf}/4\) of the mass in (33). Every such
collection obeys

\[
\frac{p_{\rm hf}}4
\le
C_{\rm cap}h^{7/4}N^{1/6}.
\tag{47}
\]

Consequently

\[
\boxed{
N_{\rm pressure}(h)
\ge
c_{\rm cap}h^{-21/2}.
}
\tag{48}
\]

The inverse-cubic source ball itself contains

\[
\boxed{
N_{\rm source}(h)
\asymp
\left(\frac R\ell\right)^3
=(RK)^3
\asymp h^{-21/2}
}
\tag{49}
\]

grid cubes. The finite-factor-frequency pressure is therefore
delocalised over the full available volume power.

In particular, taking \(N=1\) in (46) gives

\[
\sup_{m\in\mathbb Z^3}
\int_0^h\int_{Q_m}|H_h|
\le
C_{\rm cap}h^{7/4}
\longrightarrow0.
\tag{50}
\]

No translated descendant cube retains a fixed pressure payment.

## 6. Physical scale map

On the common physical trajectory, let

\[
b_j=\mathcal S_{\sigma_j}v,
\qquad
|I_j|=\sigma_j^2h_j.
\tag{51}
\]

The interaction and descendant scales are

\[
\lambda_j:=\sigma_jh_j^{-3},
\qquad
\mu_j:=\frac{\sigma_j}{K_{h_j}}
=\kappa^{-1}\sigma_j\sqrt{h_j}.
\tag{52}
\]

Their ratio is

\[
\boxed{
\frac{\lambda_j}{\mu_j}
=
\kappa h_j^{-7/2}.
}
\tag{53}
\]

The selected time layer has the fixed descendant clock

\[
\boxed{
\frac{|I_j|}{\mu_j^2}
=\kappa^2.
}
\tag{54}
\]

The inner physical coefficient dissipation satisfies

\[
\boxed{
\int_{I_j}\int_{B_{8\lambda_j}}
|\nabla v|^2\,dx\,dt
\ge c\lambda_j.
}
\tag{55}
\]

Every \(\mu_j\)-ball has dissipation at most \(C\mu_j\), so (23) becomes

\[
\boxed{
N_{\rm diss}(h_j)
\gtrsim
\frac{\lambda_j}{\mu_j}
\asymp h_j^{-7/2}.
}
\tag{56}
\]

In the finite-factor-frequency branch, (48) becomes

\[
\boxed{
N_{\rm pressure}(h_j)
\gtrsim
\left(
\frac{\lambda_j}{\mu_j}
\right)^3
\asymp h_j^{-21/2}.
}
\tag{57}
\]

Thus the two branches are:

1. a fixed fraction of the saturated local coefficient energy remains
   above the descendant frequency; or
2. a fixed finite-band pressure fraction fills the entire
   interaction-volume power in positive-clock coordinates.

## 7. Sharpness of the static exponents

The powers in the proof saturate the available static budgets.

At \(R=h^{-3}\), \(\ell\asymp\sqrt h\), and \(K\asymp h^{-1/2}\), a
weak-\(L^3\)-critical coefficient of amplitude \(R^{-1}=h^3\), occupying
volume \(R^3=h^{-9}\), and oscillating at frequency \(K\) has the ledger

\[
\|b\|_{L^{3,\infty}}\asymp1,
\tag{58}
\]

\[
\int_0^h\|\nabla b\|_2^2\,d\tau
\asymp
hK^2R
\asymp R
\asymp h^{-3}.
\tag{59}
\]

A zero-data factor with

\[
\int_0^h\|r\|_2^2\,d\tau\asymp h^3
\tag{60}
\]

then makes the Cauchy--Schwarz pressure ledger order one. The number of
\(\ell\)-cells in that reservoir is

\[
(R/\ell)^3\asymp h^{-21/2}.
\tag{61}
\]

This is a kinematic scaling ledger, not a Navier--Stokes construction
and not a proof that the pressure pairing has a nonzero sign. It shows
that weak \(L^3\), local energy, and the zero-data \(r\)-budget alone
cannot improve (48).

positive-clock scale is now disposed of. A translated
