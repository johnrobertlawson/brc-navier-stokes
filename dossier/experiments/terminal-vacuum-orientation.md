# Terminal Biot--Savart coupling permits vacuum orientation

- **Experiment:** EXP-TERMINAL-VACUUM-ORIENTATION-001
- **Route:** ROUTE-R3B
- **Status:** complete analytic counterexample and replacement reduction
- **Clay status:** unsolved
- **Input:** [projective alignment audit](projective-alignment-defect.md)

This note tests the proposed terminal coupling lemma for the selected natural
child. The lemma is false even with global Biot--Savart coupling, smooth
compactly supported finite-energy data, strong critical vorticity convergence,
and no far-field escape.

The counterexample is a sequence of Clay-admissible **instantaneous data**, not
rescalings of one Navier--Stokes trajectory and not a singular solution. It closes
only the terminal-compactness shortcut. The remaining usable information is a
zero-safe, cutoff-relative orientation tensor whose nonzero weak-* limit may live
where the limiting vorticity vanishes.

## Verdict

Let the natural-child vorticity cutoff be

\[
\eta_n=\sigma_n^{-1}\longrightarrow0.
\]

There are smooth compactly supported divergence-free velocities whose natural
rescalings satisfy, on one fixed ball,

\[
|\widehat\omega_n|=2\eta_n,
\qquad
\widehat\alpha_n=3,
\qquad
\widehat\omega_n
\longrightarrow
\widehat\omega^0
\quad\hbox{strongly in }L^{3/2},
\qquad
\widehat\omega^0=0
\]

on that ball. Their finite-band strains converge uniformly to a field \(F\) with

\[
e_1\cdot Fe_1>\frac52.
\]

Consequently, the terminal orientation measure is entirely supported over the
zero set of the limiting vorticity:

\[
\Gamma
\left(
B_1\times\{e_1\otimes e_1\}
\right)
=|B_1|,
\qquad
\widehat\omega^0\!\restriction B_1=0.
\]

The graph-support conclusion

\[
\Gamma\bigl(\{\widehat\omega^0=0\}\times\mathcal P_2\bigr)=0
\]

therefore cannot follow from any combination of terminal endpoint compactness,
finite-band convergence, far-tail tightness, and the same-snapshot Biot--Savart
identity.

The exact zero-safe replacement is

\[
\boxed{
Z_{\eta}[\omega]
=
\frac{\omega\otimes\omega}{|\omega|^2+\eta^2}
=
\frac{|\omega|^2}{|\omega|^2+\eta^2}
\xi\otimes\xi .
}
\tag{1}
\]

It obeys \(0\le Z_\eta\le I\). On the selected set
\(|\widehat\omega_n|>\eta_n\) and \(F_n:Q_n>1\),

\[
F_n:Z_{\eta_n}[\widehat\omega_n]>\frac12.
\tag{2}
\]

Thus, after restricting to the witness and taking weak-* limits, there is a
nonzero positive-semidefinite **vacuum orientation tensor**
\(\mathsf Z\) satisfying

\[
\boxed{
\int_{B_1}F:\mathsf Z\,dz\ge\frac{q_0}{2}.
}
\tag{3}
\]

Unlike \(\xi\otimes\xi\), (1) is zero-safe and remembers vorticity amplitude
relative to the collapsing cutoff. It need not be a function of the limiting
vorticity.

## 1. A compact Biot--Savart strain with a vorticity-free core

Put

\[
A
=
\operatorname{diag}
\left(3,-\frac32,-\frac32\right),
\qquad
e=e_1.
\tag{4}
\]

The linear field

\[
u_A(x)=Ax
\]

is divergence free and curl free, with symmetric strain \(A\) and

\[
e\cdot Ae=3.
\tag{5}
\]

For any divergence-free homogeneous vector field \(u\) of degree one,

\[
\nabla\times(x\times u)=-3u.
\]

Hence

\[
\Psi_u=-\frac13x\times u
\tag{6}
\]

is a vector potential for \(u\).

Choose \(\chi\in C_c^\infty(\mathbb R^3)\) with

\[
\chi=1\quad\hbox{on }B_4,
\qquad
\operatorname{supp}\chi\subset B_5,
\]

and define

\[
U=\nabla\times(\chi\Psi_{u_A}).
\tag{7}
\]

Then \(U\) is smooth, compactly supported, and divergence free. On \(B_4\),

\[
U=Ax,
\qquad
W:=\nabla\times U=0,
\qquad
S[U]=A.
\tag{8}
\]

The vorticity \(W\) is confined to the fixed annulus
\(B_5\setminus B_4\). Since \(U\) is divergence free, compactly supported, and
decays at infinity, it is exactly the Biot--Savart velocity generated by \(W\).
Thus the strain in the vorticity-free core is not an added harmonic background:
it is generated nonlocally by finite-distance vorticity.

## 2. Add a vanishing directional carrier without changing the core strain

Let

\[
v_e(x)=\frac12e\times x.
\tag{9}
\]

This rigid rotation is divergence free and satisfies

\[
S[v_e]=0,
\qquad
\nabla\times v_e=e.
\tag{10}
\]

Using (6) again, define

\[
V
=
\nabla\times(\chi\Psi_{v_e}),
\qquad
w=\nabla\times V.
\tag{11}
\]

The field \(V\) is also smooth, compactly supported, and divergence free. On
\(B_4\),

\[
V=v_e,
\qquad
w=e,
\qquad
S[V]=0.
\tag{12}
\]

For any sequence \(\sigma_n\to\infty\), set

\[
\eta_n=\sigma_n^{-1},
\qquad
\widehat u_n
=
U+2\eta_nV.
\tag{13}
\]

Its vorticity is

\[
\widehat\omega_n=W+2\eta_nw.
\tag{14}
\]

On \(B_1\), equations (8) and (12) give exactly

\[
\widehat\omega_n=2\eta_ne,
\qquad
S[\widehat u_n]=A,
\qquad
\widehat\alpha_n=3.
\tag{15}
\]

Moreover,

\[
\widehat\omega_n\longrightarrow W
\quad\hbox{strongly in }L^{3/2}(\mathbb R^3),
\tag{16}
\]

and \(W=0\) throughout \(B_4\).

## 3. The finite natural band retains the positive witness

Because \(S[U]\) is smooth and compactly supported,

\[
P_{\le M}S[U]\longrightarrow S[U]
\quad\hbox{uniformly on }B_1
\]

as \(M\to\infty\). Choose one finite \(M\) so that

\[
\left\|
P_{\le M}S[U]-A
\right\|_{L^\infty(B_1)}
<\frac14.
\tag{17}
\]

For this fixed \(M\),

\[
2\eta_n
\left\|
P_{\le M}S[V]
\right\|_{L^\infty(B_1)}
<\frac14
\tag{18}
\]

for all sufficiently large \(n\). Therefore, with

\[
F_n=P_{\le M}S[\widehat u_n],
\]

one has

\[
e\cdot F_ne
>
3-\frac14-\frac14
=\frac52
\quad\hbox{on }B_1.
\tag{19}
\]

This does not rely on choosing the band after the example. For any prescribed
finite \(M>0\), replace \(U,V\) by

\[
U_R(x)=R\,U(x/R),
\qquad
V_R(x)=R\,V(x/R)
\]

with one fixed \(R\gg M^{-1}\). Their linear core is unchanged, their transition
annulus becomes \(B_{5R}\setminus B_{4R}\), and (17)--(19) hold for that
prescribed band. The endpoint constant may depend on \(R\), but \(R\) is fixed
throughout the sequence and no vorticity escapes as \(n\to\infty\).

The low-band strains converge uniformly to

\[
F=P_{\le M}S[U]
=P_{\le M}\mathsf D[W].
\tag{20}
\]

This is the exact same-snapshot strain--vorticity coupling required by the
natural-band reduction. The limiting vorticity vanishes on the witness ball, but
its finite-distance annular part generates the limiting strain there.

## 4. Reverse natural scaling gives critical admissible snapshots

Let

\[
\ell_n=\sigma_n^{-1/2}
\]

and reverse the natural rescaling:

\[
u_n(x)
=
\ell_n^{-1}
\widehat u_n(x/\ell_n).
\tag{21}
\]

Each \(u_n\) is smooth, compactly supported, divergence free, and has finite
kinetic energy. On the physical witness ball \(\ell_nB_1\),

\[
|\omega_n|=2>1,
\qquad
\alpha_n=3\sigma_n>2\sigma_n.
\tag{22}
\]

Its critical witness content is fixed:

\[
\sigma_n^{3/2}|\ell_nB_1|=|B_1|.
\tag{23}
\]

The global endpoint norm is invariant:

\[
\|\omega_n\|_{L^{3/2,\infty}}
=
\|\widehat\omega_n\|_{L^{3/2,\infty}}
\le C.
\tag{24}
\]

The kinetic energy actually vanishes:

\[
\|u_n\|_2^2
=
\ell_n\|\widehat u_n\|_2^2
=O(\sigma_n^{-1/2}).
\tag{25}
\]

The physical finite band \(M/\ell_n=M\sqrt{\sigma_n}\) rescales exactly to the
fixed band in (19). Thus this family satisfies every static endpoint, witness,
same-velocity, finite-band, and no-escape property used by the proposed terminal
coupling lemma.

It is not one Navier--Stokes trajectory. The \(u_n\) are admissible instantaneous
data, so the construction proves that only genuine cross-time information can
exclude this vacuum orientation.

## 5. Why far-tail tightness cannot repair graph support

The vorticity generating \(F\) is not escaping to rescaled infinity. It lies in
the fixed annulus \(B_5\setminus B_4\), while the witness lies in \(B_1\).
Uniform far-tail estimates correctly retain this contribution; they do not make
the Biot--Savart operator local.

Strong critical convergence does not help either. Equation (16) is stronger than
the terminal compactness currently available, yet the polar direction of
\(2\eta_ne\) survives while its magnitude vanishes. The familiar conclusion

\[
\frac{\widehat\omega_n}{|\widehat\omega_n|}
\longrightarrow
\frac{W}{|W|}
\]

holds only where \(W\ne0\). It says nothing on the whole witness ball in this
construction.

## 6. Two-scale polar compactness

For a general natural child, write

\[
r_n=|\widehat\omega_n|,
\qquad
Q_n=\xi_n\otimes\xi_n,
\qquad
\eta_n=\sigma_n^{-1}.

Record both absolute and cutoff-relative amplitude:

\[
b_n=\frac{r_n}{1+r_n}\in[0,1],
\qquad
h_n=\frac{r_n}{r_n+\eta_n}\in[0,1].
\tag{26}
\]

On the high-vorticity witness, \(r_n>\eta_n\), so \(h_n>1/2\). Define

\[
\begin{aligned}
d\Upsilon_n(z,b,h,Q)
={}&
\mathbf1_{\widehat E_n}(z)\\
&\times
\delta_{(b_n(z),h_n(z),Q_n(z))}(db\,dh\,dQ)\,dz .
\end{aligned}
\tag{27}
\]

After a subsequence, these measures converge weak-* on

\[
\overline{B_1}
\times[0,1]
\times\left[\frac12,1\right]
\times\mathcal P_2.

The limit \(\Upsilon\) satisfies

\[
\Upsilon(\text{whole space})\ge q_0,
\qquad
\int F(z):Q\,d\Upsilon\ge q_0.
\tag{28}

The earlier orientation measure \(\Gamma\) is its \((z,Q)\)-projection. The part

\[
\Upsilon^{\mathrm{vac}}
=

\tag{29}

is the terminal vacuum orientation. In the explicit family,

\[

\qquad

\qquad

on \(B_1\). Hence all witness mass converges to

\[

\tag{30}

## 7. Minimal tensor certificate

The complete measure (27) records more information than is needed to preserve the
positive pairing. Put

\[
k_n
=
\frac{r_n}{\sqrt{r_n^2+\eta_n^2}}.
\]

Its bounded quadratic polar moment is exactly (1):

\[
Z_{\eta_n}[\widehat\omega_n]
=k_n^2Q_n.
\]

Restrict it to the witness:

\[
\mathsf Z_n
=
\mathbf1_{\widehat E_n}
Z_{\eta_n}[\widehat\omega_n].
\tag{31}
\]

The matrices \(\mathsf Z_n\) are positive semidefinite and uniformly bounded in
\(L^\infty(B_1)\). A weak-* subsequence converges to a positive-semidefinite
tensor \(\mathsf Z\). Since \(k_n^2>1/2\), (30) of the projective audit gives

\[
\int_{B_1}F_n:\mathsf Z_n\,dz
>
\frac12|\widehat E_n|
\ge
\frac{q_0}{2}.
\]

Uniform convergence of \(F_n\) proves (3). For the explicit snapshot family,

\[
\mathsf Z_n
=
\frac45e\otimes e
\quad\hbox{on }B_1,
\]

even though \(\widehat\omega_n\to0\) there.

This \(\mathsf Z\), rather than a direction obtained from
\(\widehat\omega^0\), is the smallest terminal object currently certified to
retain positive alignment.

## 8. Exact carrier-tightness criterion

The vacuum part vanishes precisely when the witness cannot approach the collapsing
amplitude axis. The required lower-tail condition is

\[
\boxed{
\lim_{\delta\downarrow0}
\limsup_{n\to\infty}
\left|
\widehat E_n
\cap
\{|\widehat\omega_n|\le\delta\}
\right|
=0.
}
\tag{32}
\]

If (32) holds, every limit (27) gives zero mass to \(\{b=0\}\). Conversely, if
(32) fails, a diagonal subsequence produces a limit with positive mass on
\(\{b=0\}\). This follows directly from

\[
b_n\le\frac{\delta}{1+\delta}
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
r_n\le\delta
\]

and weak-* compactness.

The weak-\(L^{3/2}\) endpoint controls the opposite tail:

\[
|\{r_n>R\}|\lesssim R^{-3/2}.
\]

It gives no information about (32). The compact counterexample violates (32)
even with strong \(L^{3/2}\) convergence.

## 9. Dynamic form of the surviving tensor

On \(\{r>0\}\), put

\[
\phi_\eta(r)
=
\frac{r^2}{r^2+\eta^2}.
\]

The magnitude and projective equations give

\[
D_tr
=
\alpha r+\nu\,\xi\cdot\Delta\omega,
\tag{33}

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
D_tZ_\eta
={}&
\frac{2\eta^2r}{(r^2+\eta^2)^2}(D_tr)Q\\
&+\phi_\eta(r)
\bigl[(I-Q)SQ+QS(I-Q)\bigr]\\
&+\nu\frac{r}{r^2+\eta^2}
\left[
(I-Q)\Delta\omega\otimes\xi
+\xi\otimes(I-Q)\Delta\omega
\right].
\end{aligned}
}
\tag{34}
\]

The bare \(1/r\) has been replaced by

\[
\frac{r}{r^2+\eta^2}\le\frac1{2\eta}.
\tag{35}
\]

Because \(\eta\) scales like vorticity, (34) is exactly invariant under
Navier--Stokes scaling. It is still critical as \(\eta_n\to0\); the tensor
regularisation identifies the correct defect rather than estimating it away.
Unlike the projective equation, the map
\(\omega\mapsto Z_\eta[\omega]\) is smooth at \(\omega=0\). Its parabolic chain
rule therefore gives a stronger divergence-form evolution without any
\(\Delta\omega/|\omega|\) term.

## 10. Exact consequence for ROUTE-R3B

The proposed terminal graph-support lemma is closed as false. Global Biot--Savart
coupling and far-tail tightness retain nonlocal strain; they do not force the
local vorticity carrier to survive natural rescaling. Strong critical convergence
does not change this conclusion.

The live route is now purely dynamic:

1. propagate the bounded tensor \(Z_{\eta_n}\), or the full two-scale polar

2. show that its vacuum part vanishes by proving (32), or retain a nonzero
   cutoff-scale diffusion/variation defect in its evolution;
3. obtain suitability independently; and
4. prove rigidity for the resulting nonzero ancient solution decorated by

The static family does not kill ROUTE-R3B because it is not one trajectory. It
